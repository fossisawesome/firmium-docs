# Recap & Listening Stats

Firmium keeps a private history of what you listen to and turns it into **Firmium Recap** — a set of bold, full-screen, swipeable cards summarizing your listening, similar in spirit to a year-in-review. Everything is built from a history stored on your own device. No listening data is ever sent to a server.

## How your history is recorded

Every time a track finishes playing, Firmium records it locally — the same moment it reports the play to your server. Each entry keeps the track, artist, album, genre, and how long it played, along with the time it happened. Because the names are stored alongside the IDs, Recap works fully offline, even when you're not connected to a server.

There's nothing to turn on. Your history starts building the first time you play something.

## Opening Recap

There's no sidebar or nav button for Recap — it's kept out of the way. You can open it two ways:

- **From Settings → Stats Export**, tap **View Recap**.
- **Automatically once a week.** When you open Firmium and it's been at least seven days since you last saw it, Recap appears on its own. It won't nag you more than once a week.

## The cards

Swipe sideways (or use the arrows on desktop) to move through the cards:

1. **Total listening time** — how long you spent listening, and how many tracks that was.
2. **Top tracks** — your most-played songs, by play count.
3. **Top artists** — the artists you played most.
4. **Top albums** — your most-played albums.
5. **Most played genre** — the sound that defined the period.
6. **By time of day** — how your listening splits across morning, afternoon, evening, and night.
7. **By day of week** — which days you listen most.
8. **Biggest discovery** — something you first heard recently and quickly played a lot.
9. **Streak** — your longest run of consecutive days with music, and how many days you listened overall.

## Choosing a time range

At the top of Recap you can switch the period: **7 days**, **30 days**, **3 months**, **1 year**, or **all time**. On desktop you can also pick a **custom** date range. Every card updates to match.

## Sharing

You can save a Recap card as an image to share anywhere.

- **Desktop** — the save buttons let you export the current card, or a compact summary of the whole Recap, as a PNG using a normal save dialog.
- **Android** — swipe to the card you want (the final summary card makes a great share image) and tap the share button to send it through the system share sheet.

## Exporting the raw data

If you'd rather work with the numbers yourself, **Settings → Stats Export → Export Play History** saves your complete history as a **CSV** (for spreadsheets) or **JSON** (raw structure) file. See [Settings](/settings).
